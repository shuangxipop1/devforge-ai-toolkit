## The Sandwich Generation

You're a tech worker in your 40s. You're raising kids. One or both parents are aging. And you're realizing:

- Your parents' care falls on you (culture, logistics, finances)
- Your kids need you (time, attention, money for college)
- Your job requires you (deliverables, presence, career growth)
- Your spouse needs you too
- And you're exhausted

**You're in the middle. And nobody tells you how to do this.**

## What You'll Learn

### Part 1: The Assessment (Week 1)
**Before you make any decisions, understand the reality.**

How to evaluate your parents' current situation:
- Medical reality vs. what they admit
- Financial reality vs. what they share
- The conversation nobody wants to have
- What kind of care is actually needed vs. what's convenient

**The Family Assessment Framework**
A structured way to understand everyone's needs.

### Part 2: The Options (Week 2)
**Caregiving isn't binary. There are real options.**

The spectrum of care:
- Independent living (with supports)
- In-home care (part or full-time help)
- Assisted living (when it's time)
- Memory care (for dementia)
- Family caregiving (what you can realistically do)

Understanding the costs:
- What Medicare/Medicaid actually covers
- Long-term care insurance reality
- The cost of family caregiving (it's not free)
- Financial aid you might be missing

### Part 3: The Plan (Week 3)
**Build a caregiving plan that doesn't destroy your career.**

How to distribute care among siblings (or handle being the only one):
- The family meeting framework
- Setting boundaries with parents
- Saying no to things you can't do
- Negotiating with work for flexibility

The legal foundations you need:
- Power of attorney (financial and medical)
- Living wills and directives
- Trusts (when they make sense)
- HIPAA and why it matters now

### Part 4: Your Sanity (Week 4)
**You can't pour from an empty cup.**

The burnout warning signs:
- Physical symptoms of caregiver stress
- The anger that comes from nowhere
- Depression vs. normal stress

How to protect yourself:
- Setting realistic expectations
- Building your support network
- Finding time for yourself (it's not selfish)
- When to seek outside help

### Part 5: Having The Conversation
**How to talk to aging parents about their future.**

The script that works:
- Starting the conversation early (before crisis)
- What to say when they resist help
- Handling denial ("I'm fine!")
- The conversation about driving, money, and independence

**Word-for-word scripts you can use**

### Part 6: Work and Caregiving
**How to keep your career while caregiving.**

Your legal rights:
- FMLA for caregiving (and its limits)
- ADA accommodations
- What your employer can and can't ask

Managing performance:
- How to maintain presence while caring for parents
- The flexibility conversation with your manager
- When to disclose and when to protect privacy
